01 · Inbox

One place for everything unprocessed.

Voice memo on the drive in. Forwarded email. Screenshot from a board chair. Granola transcript from a partner meeting. Four lines typed at a red light. It all goes here.

Continuous Advancement parses each input, files it to the right Person, Partnership, Commitment, or Thread, and raises anything time-sensitive. You don’t organize. You talk. It understands.

03 · Threads

The prose-rich memory of every relationship.

When you want to prepare for a call, a board meeting, or a strategy conversation, open the Thread. Every touchpoint, commitment, and signal is assembled as prose — not a feed — alongside a draft of whatever you’re about to write.

A Thread is what you wished your CRM had been all along: the story of a relationship, not a log of activities. Partnerships, donors, board members, program sites, and initiatives each have one.

Why this

What you’ve already tried,
and why it hasn’t worked.

vs  Salesforce · NPSP

Built for funnels.
You don’t have a funnel.

Salesforce was built for sales teams moving opportunities through stages. You’re not closing sales — you’re compounding trust across many relationship types, for many years. Continuous Advancement holds partnerships, donors, program participants, alumni, board members, and peer EDs as co-equal first-class objects, not variants of Lead and Opportunity.

vs  Bloomerang · LGL · RE

Donor tools know
only donors.

Donor CRMs track donor relationships. You manage donors, partners, staff, corps members, program participants, and board members in the same hour — often in the same email. Continuous Advancement treats each as a first-class relationship with its own cadence, not as rows in a Donor table.

vs  Notion · Asana · Monday

Tickets aren’t
the unit of leadership.

These are coordination tools for teams doing repeatable work. You do non-repeatable work: unique relationships, unique commitments, unique signals. Continuous Advancement is structured around prose and commitments, not tickets and stages — because the unit of your work is a thread, not a task.

vs  A chief of staff

Extraordinary —
and $200k you don’t have.

A chief of staff is the gold standard. You cannot afford one — most mission-driven orgs your size can’t. Continuous Advancement is the working memory of a chief of staff — the tracking, the surfacing, the drafting — at roughly 2% of the cost. It does not replace human judgment. It protects your judgment from getting lost in tabs.

vs  Claude · ChatGPT · Gemini

A brilliant intern
who resets every Monday.

A chat assistant is stateless. It forgets the partner you mentioned last week, the commitment you made in February, the thread that’s gone quiet. Continuous Advancement is a second brain with durable memory, structured around how an ED actually leads — with the same model power underneath, in a container that doesn’t reset.

Under the hood

A vertical object model,
not a generic table.

Continuous Advancement is built on a set of first-class typed objects that match the real shape of mission-driven leadership. Each object has typed fields and a prose layer.

Person Partnership Commitment Initiative Touchpoint Donor Corps Member Alumni Board Member Program Site Outcome

Every input — voice memo, email, meeting transcript — passes through an understanding pass that extracts structured data while preserving the prose. Nothing is flattened. Nothing is lost to a form field. The AI layer reads your inputs, your history, and your cadences, and proposes actions. You approve. Nothing sends without your hand on it.

AI layer
Claude (Anthropic) powers understanding, ranking, and drafting. Prompts are versioned. Outputs are testable. When the model improves, your system sharpens.
Your data, your database
Per-tenant Postgres with row-level security. Exportable to your own Obsidian vault if you want a personal-portability copy. No training of third-party models on your data.
Integrations as bridges
Outlook, Gmail, Granola, Google Drive, Slack via MCP bridges. Add or remove any integration without breaking the core.
Drafts, not autonomy
The system proposes. You decide. No email sends, no donor moves, no board packet ships without an explicit human pass.

Common questions

The things EDs ask us first.

Do I have to migrate off my current CRM?

No. We import what we can and coexist with what you already run. Continuous Advancement is the layer above your tools, not a replacement for the ones that work.

Is my data private?

Yes. Per-tenant Postgres with row-level security. Your data is not used to train third-party models. You can export everything at any time.

What if the AI gets it wrong?

Every AI-generated action is a draft. You approve before anything sends. The system is designed to fail visibly, not silently.

Can my board see this?

Only the parts you choose to share. Board threads and board-member profiles are first-class — you can expose a one-page prose packet or keep everything to yourself.

How is this different from just using Claude better?

Claude is a model. Continuous Advancement is memory, structure, and cadence wrapped around that model — so the power doesn’t evaporate between sessions, and so the shape of your job (not a generic chat window) is what the AI sees.

What’s the smallest org you serve?

An ED and a laptop. We built this because leading a 10-person mission-driven org is just as cognitively expensive as leading a 200-person one — with fewer people to hand things to.
